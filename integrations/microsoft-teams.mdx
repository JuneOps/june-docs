Connect Microsoft Teams with June to receive notifications as Adaptive Cards, mirror agent request activity into a Teams channel, and let employees chat with June from Teams.

<Info>
Microsoft Teams is a **communication channel**. June uses it to send notifications, post live-progress cards for agent requests, and (optionally) respond to employees who message June. It does not manage devices or import tickets.
</Info>

## What you'll accomplish

- Grant admin consent for the June app in your Microsoft tenant
- Install the June bot in the team you want to use
- Choose the team and channel June posts notifications to
- Decide whether to mirror agent request activity and enable employee chat

## Prerequisites

Before starting the integration, ensure you have:

- A **Microsoft Entra (Azure AD) admin** who can grant tenant-wide admin consent
- Permission to install apps into the Microsoft Teams team you want to use
- June organization admin permissions

<Note>
Microsoft Teams relies on your Microsoft tenant. If you have already connected **Microsoft Entra** in June, your Tenant ID is filled in automatically; otherwise it is captured during admin consent.
</Note>

## How June Works with Teams

When you connect Teams, an administrator grants consent for the June app in your tenant. June then:

- **Reads the list of teams and channels** so you can pick where notifications go
- **Maps employees to Teams users** from your directory, so notifications and chat reach the right person
- **Posts notifications and live-progress Adaptive Cards** to the channel you select
- **Optionally answers employees** who message or @mention June

### Capabilities

| Capability | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Notifications** | June posts alerts and updates as Adaptive Cards to your chosen Teams channel |

### Channel options

After connecting, you control two behaviors:

| Setting | Default | What it does |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Mirror request executions into the Teams channel** | On | Every agent request execution posts a live-progress Adaptive Card in the configured notifications channel |
| **Allow employees to chat with June via @mention** | Off | When enabled, June responds to messages and @mentions from employees in Teams |

## Setup Process

### Step 1: Grant Admin Consent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the connection from June">
    1. Log into June
    2. Navigate to **Settings** → **Integrations**
    3. Select **Microsoft Teams** from the available integrations
    4. Click **Connect** to begin the Microsoft consent flow
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve the June app">
    1. Sign in with a Microsoft account that can grant **admin consent** for your tenant
    2. Review the permissions June requests—listing teams and users, and posting messages to channels
    3. Click **Accept** to grant consent for your organization

    <Warning>
    Admin consent must be granted by a tenant administrator. If you are not an admin, the consent screen will ask you to request approval from one before you can continue.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Return to June">
    Microsoft redirects you back to June and your **Tenant ID** is recorded. The connection is now associated with your Microsoft tenant.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Install the June Bot in Your Team

The June app must be present in the specific team you want notifications in, so it has permission to post there.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add June to the team">
    1. In Microsoft Teams, open the team you want to use
    2. Go to **Manage team** → **Apps** → **More apps**
    3. Find the **June** app and add it to the team
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 3: Choose a Team and Channel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select where notifications go">
    1. In June's Teams integration settings, choose the **Team** from the dropdown
    2. Choose the **Channel** within that team for notifications
    3. Save your selection

    <Check>
    Confirm a test notification card appears in the channel you selected.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 4: Configure Behavior

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request mirroring">
    Leave **Mirror request executions into the Teams channel** on for a live feed of agent activity, or turn it off so the channel only receives notifications.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Employee chat (optional)">
    Turn on **Allow employees to chat with June via @mention** to let employees message or @mention June for help directly in Teams. Leave it off to keep June admin-only.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No teams or channels appear in the dropdown">
    **Solutions**:
    - Confirm admin consent was granted for your tenant during connection
    - Verify the connection status shows **Active**; reconnect if it doesn't
    - If you connected Microsoft Entra separately, confirm it is still connected so the Tenant ID stays valid
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Notifications fail with a permissions error (403)">
    **Symptoms**: June can list teams but posting fails

    **Solutions**:
    - The June bot must be installed in the specific team you selected—add it via **Manage team → Apps**
    - Re-select the team and channel after installing the app
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="June isn't responding to messages">
    **Solutions**:
    - Confirm **Allow employees to chat with June via @mention** is turned on
    - Make sure the June bot is installed in the team or chat where the message was sent
    - Check that the employee's directory account uses the work email June has on file
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Connection shows disconnected">
    **Solutions**:
    - Tenant consent may have been revoked in Microsoft Entra—reconnect from June's Teams settings
    - Confirm the June app hasn't been removed from your tenant or team
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Security Best Practices

- **Tenant-scoped consent**: June operates only within the tenant that granted consent, and inbound activity is allowlisted to that tenant
- **Least privilege**: June requests only the permissions needed to list teams/users and post notifications
- **Public vs. secret values**: Your Tenant ID is a public routing identifier; no long-lived secret tokens are stored—access tokens are minted on demand per tenant
- **Revoke anytime**: Remove the June app from a team, or revoke consent in Microsoft Entra, to cut off access immediately
